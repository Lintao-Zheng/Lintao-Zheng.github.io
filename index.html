I am currently a second-year master's student in Robotics at the University of Pennsylvania (UPenn). I build end-to-end robotic systems that integrate perception uncertainty, state estimation, and real-time planning/control. Recently, I developed an uncertainty-aware MPPI framework accelerated with JAX for 100 Hz online replanning under noisy vision and disturbances.

My research experience includes contributing to robot planning and control under uncertainty at the UPenn Figueroa Robotics Lab with Prof. Nadia Figueroa, and developing a teleoperation forklift system at the UPenn xLab with Prof. Rahul Mangharam.

Previously, I also gained significant experience in sensor fusion and autonomous robot vehicles, including developing a Doppler radar-based speed detection system and line-following robot vehicles during my undergraduate studies at the University of Nottingham Ningbo China (UNNC), as well as designing and building a teleoperated robot with multimodal sensors at UPenn.

My primary research interests lie in robot learning, optimal control and uncertainty-aware decision-making, focusing on learning-based motion planning, control, and perception. I aim to develop generalizable and uncertainty-aware frameworks that integrate learning, perception, and control to achieve safe, robust, and adaptive robotic autonomy in complex real-world environments.

Research Projects

Uncertainty-Aware MPPI Planning and Control for Robust Robotic Tasks

Research Assistant · Figueroa Robotics Lab · Advised by Prof. Nadia Figueroa
  • Built an MPPI-based stochastic planning and control framework for online replanning and obstacle avoidance under perception uncertainty and disturbances, optimized via parallel rollouts.
  • Integrated YOLO-based perception and injected detection uncertainty into MPPI instead of point estimates.
  • Modeled two complementary uncertainty sources:
    • Spatial uncertainty: modeled target position as a distribution to guide MPPI toward the belief, not a single detection.
    • Detection confidence: weighted the cost by YOLO confidence to avoid high-uncertainty regions.
  • Added risk-averse and exploration costs to trade off safety and information gathering.
  • Accelerated sampling and cost evaluation with JAX and maintained a stable 100 Hz control loop; improved tracking accuracy and reduced failure rates under glare/occlusion and missed detections.

Teleoperated Forklift System Integration

Research Assistant · xLab · Advised by Prof. Rahul Mangharam
  • Used CAN protocol to establish communication between the Jetson and the forklift.
  • Built Bluetooth communication to translate joystick inputs on the Jetson into control commands.
  • Achieved full remote forklift control including driving, steering, and lifting.
  • Designed and fabricated a chassis to mount the Jetson, power system, and communication hardware with a stable top-mounted camera system.

Penn Robotics Pick-and-Place Challenge (Champion) — Dynamic Pick-and-Place Control System

Team Leader · Group Project · Sep 2025 – Dec 2025
  • Built an end-to-end pick-and-place system for a Franka Panda 7-DoF arm with FK, Jacobian, and IK for autonomous grasping and stacking in a shared workspace.
  • Implemented AprilTag-based 6-DoF pose estimation and calibrated camera–end-effector–base transformations; applied bias compensation to improve real-robot localization stability.
  • Designed strategies for static and dynamic targets (coarse-to-fine static grasping; Jacobian-based joint-space sweeping + event-triggered dynamic capture).
  • Built a ROS-based sim/execution pipeline with RViz visualization/debugging; deployed the same stack on real Franka for sim-to-real transfer.
  • Validated under perception noise and dynamic disturbances; won the UPenn Pick-and-Place Challenge.

Teleoperation Robot Design and Competition Project

Team Leader · Group Project

  • Designed the Waldo system for smooth teleoperation, including servo selection and custom coding.

Multi-Sensor Fusion Odometry on Uneven Terrain

Research Assistant · UNNC · Advised by Prof. Adam Rushworth
  • Designed a robust odometry pipeline for wheeled robots on uneven terrain to mitigate vibration- and slip-induced drift.
  • Fused LiDAR, camera, and IMU measurements with a Kalman filter to reduce localization error.
  • Calibrated and benchmarked odometry using AprilTag-based pose estimates as an external reference.

MPC-Based High-Speed Dynamic Juggling with a Franka Panda

Control & Optimization Project · Advised by Prof. Michael Posa · Sep 2025 – Dec 2025
  • Built a MuJoCo simulation for high-speed Franka juggling with ball flight prediction and contact modeling.
  • Designed a three-layer control stack (high-level timing/impact, mid-level IK/MPC, low-level torque tracking via PD + gravity compensation).
  • Ran a 100 Hz closed loop and stabilized the ball into a target region within seconds across diverse initial states.
